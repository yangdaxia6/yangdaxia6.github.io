A poem dedicated to my darling Ning:

There is a girl sweet and kind,
Was never face so pleas'd my mind;
I did but see you passing by,
And yet I love you till I die.

Your gesture, motion, and your smiles,
Your wit, your voice, my heart beguiles,
Beguiles my heart, I know not why,
And yet I love you till I die.

Your free behaviour, winning looks,
Will make a lawyer burn his books;
I touch'd you not, alas! not I,
And yet I love you till I die.

Had I your fast betwixt mine arms,
Judge you that think such sports were harms,
Were't any harm? no, no, fie, fie,
For I will love you till I die.

Should I remain confined there,
So long as Phoebus in his sphere,
I to request, she to deny,
Yet would I love you till I die.

Cupid is winged and doth range,
Your country so my love doth change:
But change you earth, or change you sky,
Yet will I love you till I die.
